About the lab

Science in service of patients

Founded in 2012, Apex CRL has grown from a small translational team into a multi-disciplinary research program with a shared commitment to rigorous, reproducible, patient-centered science.

Our mission

Built on curiosity. Guided by evidence.

Apex CRL was established to close the gap between clinical observation and actionable medical knowledge. We believe the most important questions in medicine begin at the bedside — and that answering them requires equal parts methodological rigor, collaborative spirit, and genuine curiosity about why patients differ.

Our work spans Phase II–IV clinical trials, observational cohort studies, biomarker discovery, and health outcomes research. We partner with clinicians, statisticians, and basic scientists to ensure our findings are both mechanistically grounded and clinically relevant.

What guides us

Core values

Reproducibility first

We pre-register trials, share data and code, and hold ourselves to standards we would apply to others' work.

Mentorship as science

Training the next generation of clinical researchers is as central to our mission as publishing findings.

Equity in research design

We actively design studies to include under-represented populations and interrogate findings for differential effects.

Collaboration over competition

The most durable scientific contributions emerge from open exchange — with collaborators, trainees, and peer reviewers.

Timeline

How we got here

2012 — Lab founded

Dr. M. Reyes establishes Apex CRL with a focus on cardiovascular biomarkers and a team of four.

2015 — First NIH R01

Landmark funding enables the APEX-HEART cohort study, enrolling 1,200 patients over three years.

2018 — Precision medicine expansion

New genomics core and biobank infrastructure allow integration of multi-omics data into clinical studies.

2022 — Neurology program launch

Collaboration with the Department of Neurology inaugurates a cross-disciplinary cognitive outcomes research program.

2026 — Today

28 lab members, 14 active studies, and over 320 peer-reviewed publications.
